The House at Clearview Peak

Situated in the lonely wilderness of Northern Maine, the House at Clearview Peak once served as a vacation home to its wealthy owners. Built at a time when towers and ostentatious decorative trim were still in-vogue, the structure has since fallen into disrepair--the crumbling brickwork and rotting wood balustrades suggest that its last inhabitant, Tarquin McAllister, did little in the way of upkeep. An eccentric millionaire, McAllister took up residence in the old vacation home after spending years travelling abroad. Locals report that he mostly kept to himself, occasionally dispatching his servants into town to procure whatever odds and ends he needed. Some of the more superstitious townsfolk also whisper of unusual visitors from distant lands bringing bizarre souvenirs and artifacts to McAllister’s home, though most of these stories remain unsubstantiated rumors.

Clearview Peak--ironically named--is perennially blanketed in a dense fog. The drive up the mountain feels increasingly perilous as the paved road gives way to an unkempt dirt path. Upon passing the gates leading up to the property, the house looms into view: tall and dark, shrouded in mist and creaking under its own weight. Light shines from a few of the first floor windows. It seems someone has already arrived.

Choose Your Hero

Heir

As you are the late Tarquin McAllister's only living next of kin, his estate has fallen into your possession. You have decided to take stock of the house and its contents to see whether it can be renovated and sold.

Engineer

You’ve been hired by the heir to help survey the property and determine whether it should be renovated or torn down. Just looking at the property, you're pretty sure you aren't being paid enough for this gig.

Detective

Investigating a series of cold cases and missing persons reports have lead you to the House at Clearview Peak. You have brought a warrant and your partner, the police officer, in the hopes of discovering additional leads.

Police Officer

You are the brawn to the detective’s brains--sure, you might not be the sharpest tool in the shed, but no tool is sharper than Justice.**

**("Justice" is the name of your billyclub. So technically, most tools are sharper than Justice.)
